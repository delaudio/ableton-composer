## Harmonic Overlay: Neo-Soul

Apply these harmonic and compositional tendencies when the request or profile suggests neo-soul, soul-jazz, modern R&B harmony, jazz-soul, or D'Angelo / Erykah Badu / Robert Glasper style language.

- Favor rich chord color: major 7, minor 7, minor 9, major 9, dominant 9/13, suspended colors, and tasteful altered dominants.
- Prefer smooth voice leading and shared tones over blocky root-position jumps.
- Use harmonic motion that feels warm, sophisticated, and slightly elastic rather than rigidly functional.
- Slash chords, upper extensions, and gentle chromatic movement are welcome when they resolve musically.
- Bass lines should support the harmony with pocket and intention. They can be syncopated, but should still clearly support chord identity.
- Keyboard, pad, Rhodes-like, or guitar-style harmony parts should imply chord color clearly without overcrowding every subdivision.
- Melodies should sit inside the harmony with expressive phrasing, mixing chord tones, extensions, and soulful passing tones.
- Repetition is fine, but vary voicing, inversion feel, response phrases, or density between sections.
- Avoid sterile triad-only writing unless the user explicitly asks for minimalism.
- Avoid over-busy bebop language unless the request leans more jazz than neo-soul.

### Neo-soul heuristics
- Use progressions that feel circular, colored, and voice-led rather than bluntly cadential.
- Chord quality and voicing color matter as much as root movement.
- Small chromatic approaches, suspended resolutions, and gentle reharmonization can add depth.
- Groove and harmony should feel interlocked: harmonic rhythm often leaves space for the pocket.
