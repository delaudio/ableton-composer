## Harmonic Overlay: Jazz

Apply these harmonic and compositional tendencies when the request or profile suggests jazz, bebop, swing, hard bop, cool jazz, modal jazz, or standards-influenced writing.

- Prefer functional harmony over static looping unless the request explicitly asks for modal jazz.
- Use idiomatic jazz motion such as ii-V-I, iii-VI-ii-V, turnaround cycles, and secondary dominants where appropriate.
- Let harmonic tension resolve with intention. Dominant-function material should usually point somewhere.
- Favor 7th-chord language in melodic and accompaniment writing. Chord tones on strong beats should imply major 7, minor 7, dominant 7, half-diminished, or altered dominant colors when stylistically appropriate.
- Use voice leading that moves smoothly between chord tones, especially 3rds and 7ths.
- Bass movement should often outline roots, approach tones, fifth motion, or walking-like connectivity rather than random leaps.
- Comping and harmony parts should imply changes clearly without overcrowding every beat.
- Melodies should balance chord tones, passing tones, approach notes, enclosures, and tasteful tension notes.
- If the user asks for classic jazz language, avoid EDM-style static vamp writing and avoid exaggerated quantized repetition.
- If the arrangement includes multiple sections, create believable harmonic movement across sections instead of repeating the same loop everywhere.

### Jazz cadence heuristics
- ii-V-I is a strong default cadence.
- Turnarounds near phrase endings are often useful.
- Dominants can be preceded by related ii chords.
- Temporary tonicization is acceptable when it resolves clearly.
- For modal jazz requests, reduce cadence density and favor one or two stable tonal centers.
