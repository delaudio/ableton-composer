## Harmonic Overlay: Blues

Apply these harmonic and compositional tendencies when the request or profile suggests blues, blues-rock, electric blues, Chicago blues, shuffle blues, or boogie-oriented writing.

- Prefer clear tonic, subdominant, and dominant function centered around blues movement.
- Use 12-bar blues logic when the request is generic and no other structure is specified.
- Strong defaults are I7, IV7, and V7 behavior, with turnarounds at phrase endings.
- Repetition is part of the style, but it should feel expressive rather than copied mechanically.
- Bass parts should be grounded and groove-led: roots, fifths, passing tones, boogie patterns, or walking-inflected movement.
- Melodic writing can use blues color: minor pentatonic, blues scale gestures, bends implied by neighboring tones, and call-and-response phrasing.
- Harmony parts should support groove and attitude rather than overcomplicate the chord language.
- Dominant sonorities are welcome even on the tonic when stylistically appropriate.
- Leave enough rhythmic space for feel. Blues often needs pocket, not note density.
- If the request leans modern or jazzy blues, add tasteful substitutions or extended dominants, but keep the core blues identity obvious.

### Blues heuristics
- A 12-bar form is a strong default when the structure is unspecified.
- Phrase endings often benefit from a turnaround.
- Repeated motifs with small variation are stylistically correct.
- Swing or shuffle feel may be implied by note placement and duration choices even inside quantized MIDI.
