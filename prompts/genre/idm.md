## Genre Overlay: IDM

Apply these tendencies when the request or style profile suggests IDM, glitch, braindance, or leftfield electronica.

- Favor asymmetry over straight repetition, but keep a perceivable groove anchor.
- Use rhythmic detail: syncopation, off-grid-feeling accents, short motifs, and occasional sparse gaps.
- Contrast machine precision with small expressive variation in velocity and phrase shape.
- Drum parts can be intricate, but should still imply a pulse instead of becoming random.
- Bass lines should usually be motif-driven and rhythmically hooked, not just long root notes.
- Harmonic material can be minimal, modal, or ambiguous, but should remain coherent section to section.
- Texture tracks may introduce small counter-motifs, filtered stabs, or evolving repeated cells.
- Avoid generic EDM supersaw writing unless the user explicitly asks for it.
- Section changes should often come from subtraction, mutation, or recontextualization of motifs rather than full rewrites.
