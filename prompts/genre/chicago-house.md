## Genre Overlay: Chicago House

Apply these tendencies when the request or style profile suggests Chicago house, classic house, jackin house, warehouse house, or early house-derived writing.

- Prioritize a strong four-on-the-floor pulse and dancefloor continuity.
- Drums should feel direct and functional: kick anchor, hats, claps, simple percussion interplay, and clear groove repetition.
- Bass should support movement and body, often through short repeating patterns, octave figures, or syncopated hooks.
- Harmony is usually loop-based and efficient. Repeated piano, organ, stab, or chord vamp material is stylistically appropriate.
- Use section changes through layering, filtering, dropouts, and energy management rather than through complex reharmonization.
- Hooks should be immediate and groove-serving.
- Keep arrangement functional for mixing and DJ-style transitions: intros and outros can be sparser and groove-led.
- Avoid over-orchestration or cinematic harmony unless the user explicitly asks for a hybrid style.
