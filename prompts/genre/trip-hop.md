## Genre Overlay: Trip-Hop

Apply these tendencies when the request or style profile suggests trip-hop, downtempo noir, Bristol-style beats, smoky hip-hop ambience, or moody breakbeat-driven writing.

- Favor slower tempos, head-nod pocket, and a slightly dragged emotional feel.
- Drums should feel weighty, dusty, and characterful rather than hyper-clean or festival-polished.
- Bass lines should lock with the kick and often feel hypnotic, restrained, and motif-based.
- Harmonic material can be dark, modal, melancholic, or suspended, but should stay memorable and repeatable.
- Texture is important: pads, keys, vinyl-like motifs, filtered chords, and sparse melodic hooks are useful.
- Leave space. Trip-hop often feels stronger when arrangement density is controlled.
- Repetition is a feature, but sections should evolve through subtraction, texture changes, and small phrase mutations.
- Avoid maximal EDM-style builds unless the user explicitly asks for them.
- If a lead melody exists, it should feel evocative and understated rather than virtuosic.
